I originally planned for my usual east/west Japan itinerary but as I was adding Takayama and Kanazawa to my "next time in Japan" wishlist (that's how cracked out I am I'll be making a next next trip wishlist before even leaving for this one) I realized that this was as a good a chance to go for it.

So I did. A month ago in December I swapped a bunch of trains and stays and now I'm doing Yokohama -> Nagoya -> Takayama -> Kanazawa -> Nagano -> Tokyo. Five new cities and with them five new prefectures. As much as I love Onomichi I'm proud of myself for letting go and going somewhere new especially since I can't even run the Shimanami Kaido like I would normally.

Yokohama is basically a post flight short stop. Get in, eat dinner, go to sleep, head out the next morning. This trip I'm going to try to do my runs on my travel days. Will make things a bit hurried but will get to recover from the run while I'm sitting on the train. Won't risk too many daily steps either with a full day of exploring. Anyways I should be able to run to the waterfront and back and see what that's like.

Shinkansen to Nagoya. Very glad I did the Yokohama buffer night and shinkansens instead of flights so I don't need to worry about my flight being an hour behind. Looking forward to regional Nagoya foods: miso katsu, ogura toast, hitsumabushi, chicken wings, flat udon. And maybe a Toyota car museum visit. I think this will more or less the same thing.
