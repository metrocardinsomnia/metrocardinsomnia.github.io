So one of my big goals this trip was going to Hokkaido. Until now I've never even gone north of Tokyo. 

![20260507_203542-COLLAGE.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/801a2c67-6c3a-4de7-a1c1-bc140ae25c7c)

> cycling route birch trees, riverside trail


I got in via a 1 hour 50 minute flight from Okayama which was $104 via ANA. As usual, Japanese domestic flights go super smooth. Maybe because so many people travel via shinkansen (which I prefer too), domestic terminals are always super tiny and you can get to your gate in like five minutes total. 

First thing I realized when I arrived in Sapporo was that it was cold. I'd spent the last week or so in the Setonaikai area biking past citrus trees, running along the beach, walking around in sandals in high 60s and low 70s weather and that first Sapporo night was high 30s and cold. Luckily I was able to layer up. And after that first night, it warmed up the next two days to 40s and 50s so it was alright.

And in fact, the cold brought two benefits: (1) encouraged checking out the massive underground "Pole Town" mall that runs along the Namboku line between Sapporo station, Odori, and Susukino and (2) there were still cherry blossoms. 

![20260507_203441-COLLAGE.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/31b2800c-0e98-4605-bfcb-3ec14a1240b2)

> cherry blossoms; smooch coffee

The underground mall was really cool. I can imagine it's great in the winter when there's a ton of snow and you can still walk and check out small shops and hang out with your friends there. But also good for when it's rainy or cold or whatever. I went to the Kinotoya bake cheese tart shop there at least once per day; those cheese tarts were hot and fresh and so good. Their soft serve too was the creamiest milkiest soft cream of any place I've had (and gigantic, definitely a lactose threat). 

![20260507_203524-COLLAGE.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ae00cbd5-140c-4311-ab68-f0b9e3375502)

> kinotoya soft cream, "omelette parfait" (really just sponge cake), bake cheese tart

I stayed in the Susukino area which I think is the default recommendation since that's where most of the stuff to do is and yeah I would recommend it too.

Sapporo has a few meibutsu regional foods: "Genghis Khan" aka lamb bbq, miso ramen, and soup curry. And just dairy products in general. And corn and seafood. I think those are the main ones. 

Out of all of those, dairy was my favorite (see cheese tart and soft cream above). I did not try "Genghis Khan". I thought miso ramen was ok - very oily (which is intentional and I get it) but also at this point I've learned and acknowledged that ramen is my least favorite Japanese noodle dish. Definitely some "skill issue" going on here haha but I always burn my mouth and splatter soup all over myself. And have never really liked how salty the broth is. I way prefer tsukemen, mazesoba/aburasoba, udon, soba, literally all others to ramen. 

![20260507_203459-COLLAGE.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a0e47008-62bc-4844-b3ed-e512152724ff)

> miso ramen (sumire); soup curry sama

And for soup curry, I had that once in Tokyo and similar to here it's like.. ok? I wish they put noodles in it to be closer to say khao soi rather than serving it alongside rice. I think I'd rather eat normal Japanese curry rice or khao soi. 

Ok that's enough hating haha. I did have corn soft cream in Odori park and that was good. And also did a sushi place and they had this torched whitefish nigiri that was amazing. 

![20260507_203558-COLLAGE.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c5c68158-cf51-4c5e-a93b-fe934fe64589)

Sapporo felt a bit like Denver or Salt Lake City? Maybe a bit of Chicago too? Really wide streets. Grid pattern. Cold with mountains in the distance. Nature close by too. 

![20260507_203316-COLLAGE.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/db418a0d-99cb-47e4-b3a9-858b6ed2d8f8)

> nikka whiskey sign; street with mountains in the distance

I didn't actually leave the city though - requires a car and/or motivation and I was too lazy to do that. But some common day trips include going out to some lakes or hikes or even just the Nikka distillery past Otaru but the free guided tour requires reservations a month out and I hate making reservations. 

![20260507_203735-COLLAGE.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/54665a90-35ce-4f1a-a4f5-0854fe5a8321)

> corn soft cream in odori park; stamp

Sake (realizing I should just do a beverage post or even a sake one and tea one) - I did get to try two sorta bucket list sakes at this one place. But honestly I didn't love any of the places I went to in Sapporo the same way I did tachinomis in Okayama, Takamatsu, and Onomichi. I was talking to my friend from Korea in Aomori about this but I just have a much higher success rate in the smaller cities. Fewer options yes, but those spots are also more chill? Easier to start and continue conversations in? 

![20260507_203621-COLLAGE.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6b8c5579-053e-49d9-9ef4-9546ccd44d2f)

> ubusuna 5 noujou + jikon omachi

I didn't stay in Hakodate (went straight to Aomori) but next time I might do that for either Onuma park or Mt. Hakodate hikes. Those both look fun and are easily accessible via public transit. 

Overall Sapporo was good and I'm glad I finally came up and checked it out. But I currently would rather continue visiting new prefectures (think I'm at 18 out of 47 now) over repeating Hokkaido.
