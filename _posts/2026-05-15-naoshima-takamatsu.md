Takamatsu, the home of sanuki udon, and Naoshima art island. My favorite new place this trip. A memory I want to go back to already. 

![PXL_20260421_030159511.MP.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/00fc802c-cbeb-4fa0-b7f5-42d0cd7078d4)

> kabocha, yayoi kusama

I love udon. You can have it for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. You can find it for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. You can have it cold, you can have it hot. You can have it the day after throwing up your whole stomach twice from yakitori food poisoning and be fine, perhaps almost happy about food again. 

And of course you can have it in Takamatsu, the home of sanuki udon.

![20260515_201745-COLLAGE.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/4415779d-b4ee-4345-9373-a713e6149ae4)

> kashiwa udon (chicken karaage sorta), niku udon 

While I do love hot niku udon (especially the one from Makino Udon in the basement of Hakata Bus Terminal in Fukuoka) to fully appreciate the texture of Kagawa prefecture sanuki udon you should try some cold. This guy in my kitchen knife discord recommended (more like said I'd be fined if I went to Takamatsu and did not go to) Setobare Udon about 8km east of JR Takamatsu so I decided to run the 8-10km out there in the morning.

I got there maybe 8:40 and was 13th in line before open. Though just like Portillos, a staff member came out and took our orders so that by the time you're seated your food's almost ready. 

My photos are terrible but the udon and tempura were both exceptional. That eggplant tempura was so incredibly juicy and yet still crisp and light at the same time. And the udon! You can see the noodles themselves shine. Each bite simultaneously chewy yet still soft. Chef himself is in the front of the restaurant kneading and cutting and cooking the udon.

![20260515_201617-COLLAGE.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/24a582c1-0220-49dd-b2e0-30737e7adc97)

> setobare udon, hand cut 100% domestic wheat udon perhaps the best meal this trip...

That evening before dinner I was looking for a standing bar to hang out at and Stand Bar Kotoko was nearby so I went. So best udon of the trip and then couple that with best sake of the trip. As usual I like to try local sake from the prefecture (jizake) which would be the yorokobi gaijin on the left.

But then this one guy came in - like really tall, well-dressed, well-spoken, hair back in a bun, and to the owner he was like, "as soon as I saw you post this on SNS I had to come and drink it" and asked for one of the limited time specials.

I feel like in life there's people with taste and people without. But that's ok because even if you have no idea what you're doing like me, you can just ask for what someone else is having and change your life that way. 

And that's what happened because Ubusuna from Hananoka brewery in Kumamoto is the best sake I've ever had in my life. Slight amount of natural carbonation. So balanced, smooth, fruity, kinda yakult like, I can't accurately describe. I just want to drink more. (Which I did by going back the next day). 

![20260515_200758-COLLAGE.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/3a97f9b6-cb19-42b5-81cb-56fa16d806af)

> yorokobi gaijin and ubusuna 2 noujou

Well before I went back I had to do something during the daytime so I did my daytrip to Naoshima art island about an hour's ferry ride away.

I recommend getting on the ferry a bit early so you can get good window seats. And also buy a return trip ticket so that when you're trying to come back you're already set.

You can reserve museum entry in advance which I probably should have done for one of them that sold out but honestly I hate making reservations and have a much better time just running around and exploring and just going to whatever.

![20260515_200853-COLLAGE.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/366a6e65-9366-4387-81d4-5e3d495d756c)

> coffee and the coast

You can rent a bike on the island or take a shuttle or local bus around but being me I decided I'd just run and it was the perfect day for it. Blue skies, good breeze, perfect day for shorts and a longsleeve.

I decided on a clockwise loop so that running on the lefthand side of the road I'd always be along the sea. 

![20260515_200836-COLLAGE.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/e71c8f13-c800-4615-ba58-1c60198bbff3)

> a local artist makes froggies out of discarded fishing buoys, so cute

Most (all?) the museums are modern art and feature buildings designed by Tadao Ando who is my favorite architect so I had a good time that day. 

Some of the museums are just normal museusm but there are a few tiny "art house project" ones where it's like 10 people every 15 minutes and tehre's even one that's only one person at a time. I did the 10 person one which involves a pitch black room and that was super cool. You do need to buy tickets in advance but I just ran there and bought them via QR code for the next time slot and that worked out just fine. 

![20260515_202152-COLLAGE.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c5ecc9d1-ad5d-4c8a-bbde-65c2507d0c5c)

The Naoshima New Museum of Art had a nice little terrace cafe so I got some wakoucha and a chocolate tart. Look at how pretty that tea is in porcelain.

![20260515_195826-COLLAGE.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/d6e7055d-312e-417c-9204-7675a6782285)

Running down some of those downhill slopes was amazing. Must be even better on bike.

![PXL_20260421_035354918.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/3c15783d-b5db-4291-8297-12a05a7e8fe1)

Oh I had the sweetest interaction too. There's this local recycling center sorta thing - basically like a thrift store where locals donate things but everything is free. Purely for reuse and sharing. What a cool concept. I chatted with the staff lady for a bit talking about whatever and then this mother and daughter came in to drop off a bunch of stuff and I talked to them too. The mother showed me photos from hiking near Mt Fuji in Yamanashi and between those and watching Yuru Camp I really need to go there next.

Anyways they dropped off a bunch of stuff including a cute Hokkaido shima enaga bird plush that I accepted and left a note for. Ended up chatting with them on the way to the ferry since they were going back to Takamatsu too.

![PXL_20260421_021346610.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ebbb93c5-861a-41f9-96d8-0bd9c2d96429)

And then I had more Ubusuna (this was the 1 noujou whereas first night was 2 noujou) and ro man (not "kuchi man" as I said at first haha mixing up kanji and katakana and not knowing anything about sake brands/meigara). 

Stand Bar Kotoko was such a good place, especially going two days in a row and being able to chat with the owner (she was so nice) and other customers who I recognized from the day before (guy who runs after work and stops by for Heartland beer which I approve of - definitely my favorite Japanese beer; oh and sake connoisseur guy was there too and I asked him about his favorite sake producing prefectures and wrote them down somewhere but forgot but I think it was Niigata, Akita, Nagano? Or maybe Yamagata? I should check..).
