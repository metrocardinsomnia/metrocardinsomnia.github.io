I'm currently in Okayama. The ojiisans at the standing bar in Takamatsu and people online said there'd be nothing to do here and they are sorta right but that's ok.

![PXL_20260420_085319471.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/2714c5db-0f47-4b35-bd88-17e4369653e3)

> ubusuna from Hananoka brewery in Kumamoto | at stand bar kotoko in Takamatsu 




Today it's raining anyways and that just means going to museums and doing coin laundry and chilling in bed next to the window and writing.

And last night was still fun. Post dinner I stopped by a standing bar here, Stand Bar Retrie run by Ai-chan. I talked to the regulars about running and spinach and local restaurants and Takamatsu and I don't know what else and it was all just so warm and welcoming and friendly. 

![PXL_20260422_115746522.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/d732f819-c9f8-4ce3-8488-34ab4a47abaa)



> nikka taketsuru 竹鶴 at stand bar retrie in okayama


At this point I'm convinced the best way for me to have a good time (other than running by the sea in the morning) is to find any 立ち飲み ("tachinomi" aka standing bar) and just hang out and talk to people for an hour or two. Guaranteed good time and I think the perfect way to end a day when traveling solo.


Some people drop in for a single beer after a post-work. In Takamatsu there was this one guy who was really tall and very well dressed with his hair in a bun and I basically just asked for whatever sake he got and it was amazing and also asked him what prefectures and breweries he liked. Ubusuna from Hananoka brewery in Kumamoto is amazing by the way! Probably the best sake I've had so far; the slight carbonation and just pure cleanliness and smoothness was incredible to the point you're simply tasting the flavor of the sake like there's no alcohol there at all.

![PXL_20260418_083106349.jpg](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/03e2d830-607a-48fd-845c-02da0e5ef28f)

> asahi akahoshi (red star) from shirakami shouten in onomichi

There was this other standing bar in Onomichi shoutengai, 白神商店 opened just one year ago since I don't remember seeing it in Onomichi before. The owner Shirakami-san spoke really good Chinese since he studied it in college like thirty years ago and had been to more places in China than I have so on my first visit when it was just me I talked to him a lot in Chinese and on the subsequent night also had a bunch of fun talking to various locals stopping by after work and these two girls visiting from Shiga prefecture. 

Izakaya are great too but tachinomi suit me better. No seating charge, more casual, and an implicit assumption than anyone there is open and happy to chat with you. And in the lulls the owner is there too always flowing in between people and conversation. It is a wonderful thing.
