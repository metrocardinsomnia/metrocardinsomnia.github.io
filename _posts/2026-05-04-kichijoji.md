I'm going home tomorrow. 

Still remember when I was in Shizuoka and overbuying tea and realizing uh oh I'm only at stop two out of nine and need to leave room for future shopping.

And now even that feels like a while ago. Three week+ trip. It has been wonderful. 

With its highs and lows for sure but honestly the best part has been the daily loop. Wake up whenever. Run in the morning (or don't). Shower. Go wander around and find somewhere for lunch. Come back in the afternoon to nap or play Steam Deck in bed. Doodle around until dinner and then find somewhere to go. Maybe start thinking about the next city, maybe do laundry in the hotel. A lot of "maybes" and no plans and it's truly wonderful.

Though I guess I can give some highs and lows too. 

Highs would be: making a friend from Korea in Aomori and hanging out for a day! There is such a big difference between talking to staff at a coffee shop/bar/other customers/etc. vs just hanging out with a friend. 

Favorite new place would definitely be Takamatsu plus Naoshima day trip. So much good udon plus Seto Inland Sea vibes. 

Favorite new cafe experience would be Tea Stand Yamate in Onomichi, favorite standing bar convos would be Stand Bar retrie in Okayama (ty Ai-chan), and favorite sake would be Ubusuna at Stand Bar Kotoko in Takamatsu.

Lowlight of the trip would be yakitori food poisoning on my last night in Sendai and then throwing up in the hotel in Kichijoji and then making the poor decision to still go to my omakase reservation and getting super lightheaded and throwing up in their bathroom (omg so glad I did not throw up anywhere else can you imagine) though I am thankful that did not go worse and the chef and staff there were so nice and even offered to pack me a bento with the remaining courses when I asked to excuse myself to escape to my hotel. 

But back to Kichijoji. Which I haven't really talked about at all other than in the title but whenever I was planning this trip in February or whatever I decided to come here rather than the usual Shibuya-adjacent Nakameguro or elsewhere. I think my reasons were that this area would be (1) new and (2) much quieter while still (3) having enough stuff to do. When I was coming in from Sendai I started having my doubts seeing that it would take like an hour to get to a pudding place in Ueno I wanted to visit. But I ended up getting off the Shinkansen a stop early and doing that on the way in which was a great decision.

Because that meant the last three days were all just chilling here in Kichijoji next to Inokashira Park. Well chilling and lying in bed with my stomach hurting too. But all the assorted shopping I wanted to do was here anyways. Even found a cool taiwanese tea place and plenty of other good stuff here too. Might even intentionally pick this neighborhood again in the future. 

Today it was seventies (20+ centigrade) and very windy and just a great day to walk around in the Kichijoji backstreets. Actually a great day to admire outfit coordination too haha as I tried to figure out what remaining uniqlo things to buy. There was this flower shop owner guy just wearing black shoes, faded straight fit black jeans, and a darkish denim coverall jacket and that just looked so good. So natural. 

Gonna keep trying.
