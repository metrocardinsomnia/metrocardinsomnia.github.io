Today is the beginning of a month-ish in Fenway. I don't know what to call it. It's an away rotation for Michelle, but for me? Not a staycation, not a retreat. Perhaps a working holiday. A residency if we didn't already associate that word with so much else.

Anyways, today's breakfast is two soft boiled eggs (not jammy enough) and some Fujian dahongpao rock oolong. In the background we have the window AC and some Osakan hot 100 radio going. Dang I've been spoiled by fancy apartment buildings for so long but window AC and furnace take me back to Duke dorm days. Except those window ACs were way moldier and the furnace didn't have a box around it to keep you from burning yourself. 

Japanese learning status check:
- Kanji memorization / writing --> 1342 Kanji in Ringotan! 1006 already = grade 6 Kyoiku kanji. Next goal is all 2136 Joyo Kanji which gets you to grade 9. Also just tangibly, yesterday I felt like I could read almost all the comments in the food related YouTube videos I was watching without needing to look that much stuff up. Nice.
- Reading --> finished all 16 volumes of Yotsuba maybe 1 month ago. Currently at page 26 of Mieko Kawakami's 黄色の家 (Sisters in Yellow) which I got from Tsubaya in Shizuoka in April. The last few pages of pure dialogue went really fast. 
- "Speaking" --> well not doing any really but am journaling in junky Japanese; grammar and formality are super rough still for sure. Ok right during father's day dim sum I talked to my father-in-law's Japanese coworkers about Sendai oysters and Naoshima museums and running marathons in Japanese, that was fun. 
- Listening --> "yes"; not measuring anything but have been listening to J-Wave Midday Lounge on radiko and then watching Tamori-san's 空耳アウー、Kasso skateboarding, 孤独のグルメ Solitary Gourmet, and just random San Francisco vlogs to find ideas for September.
